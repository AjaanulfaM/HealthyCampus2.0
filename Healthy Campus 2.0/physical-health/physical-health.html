Physical Health



Benefits of having good Physical Health


Given the overwhelming evidence, it is clear that engaging in regular physical activity is crucial for maintaining good health and leading a fulfilling life into old age. The health benefits of consistent exercise are well-documented, emphasising its essential role in preventing many serious health conditions.


Regular physical activity has been proven to significantly lower the risk of:


Heart Disease

  • Leading cause of death worldwide.
  • Often associated with high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and smoking.
  • Can lead to heart attacks, strokes, and other serious conditions.

Cancer

  • A group of diseases involving abnormal cell growth with the potential to invade or spread to other parts of the body.
  • Common types include breast cancer, lung cancer, and prostate cancer.
  • Treatment options include surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation therapy.

Diabetes

  • A chronic condition characterized by high blood sugar levels.
  • Type 1 diabetes involves insufficient insulin production, while Type 2 involves insulin resistance.
  • Can lead to serious complications like heart disease, kidney failure, and nerve damage.

Dementia

  • A general term for a decline in cognitive function severe enough to interfere with daily life.
  • Alzheimer's disease is the most common cause of dementia.
  • Symptoms include memory loss, confusion, and difficulty with problem-solving.

Osteoarthritis

  • A degenerative joint disease causing pain, swelling, and reduced motion in joints.
  • Commonly affects knees, hips, and hands.
  • Risk factors include aging, joint injury, and obesity.

Depression

  • A common mental disorder characterized by persistent sadness and loss of interest or pleasure in activities.
  • Can lead to a variety of emotional and physical problems.
  • Treatment options include medication, therapy, and lifestyle changes.
